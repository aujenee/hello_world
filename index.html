The USWNT (& Potential Recruits)

Starting Up Top

Going into the final game of the 2019 Women's World Cup against the Netherlands, the starting forward lineup was composed of Tobin Heath, Alex Morgan & Megan Rapinoe. Below are their USWNT stats, career highlights and other influential forwards who performed in the World Cup finale.

The World Cup Final Forwards

#17. Tobin Heath (Portland Thorns FC)

"Tobin Heath of USA takes a photo with the France 2019 Trophy" from Fifa.com

    Appearances in game: 156
    Career Goals: 30
    Assists: 38
    World Cups: 2

Beyond soccer, Tobin Heath has partnered with her teammates Christen Press, Meghan Klingenberg and Megan Rapinoe to form a clothing company dedicated to challenging "the status quo". Their company, Re-inc, enforces inclusivity regarding gender neutrality.

#13. Alex Morgan (Orland Pride)

Michael Chow from USA Today Sports

    Appearances in game: 169
    Career Goals: 107
    Assists: 43
    World Cups: 2

One of the most known players in women's soccer, Alex Morgan is heavily contributed with bringing attention to the underrated sport through her talent and skill. She recently announced her first pregancy and will be missing this year's preseason in Florida.

#15. Megan Rapinoe (Reign FC)

Michael Chow from USA Today Sports"

    Appearances in game: 158
    Career Goals: 50
    Assists: 59
    World Cups: 2

Known for her iconic 2019 season and activism, Megan Rapinoe is a catalyst of social change in the sports industry and strong advocate of gender equality. In 2019, she recieved the Best FIFA Women's Player Award and lead her team in refusing to visit the White House, as is customary of American teams following their success.
